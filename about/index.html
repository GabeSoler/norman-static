About

Since we'll be working together for a while, it's only fair to introduce myself a little. I am a Dutch national who came to Scotland for its hills, education and never-failing hospitality. My interest in psychotherapy emerged during my time studying anthropology as I became aware of the role our culture plays in shaping what we feel, and are allowed to feel.

This interest led me to volunteer as a befriender and for mental health helplines, eventually doing a certificate degree in Interpersonal Dialogue counselling from the University of Edinburgh, and a postgraduate degree in Psychodynamic counselling at Human Development Scotland. The reason for focusing on the psychodynamic approach is both an intellectual appreciation of originating schools of thought, and personally recognizing psychodynamic phenomena early in life and having to fervently google to understand that the unconscious was at play. I explored psychodynamic thinking deeply through a Masters in Psychoanalytic Studies at Birkbeck, London.

Besides that, I have practiced meditation since the age of 15 to address my own neurodivergence, eventually doing teacher training in Tibetan yoga and extended meditation retreats. I actively continue my study of both contemplative practice and therapy under supervision.
