Why Therapy?

Whether you're going through a crisis, want to tackle long-standing difficulties, or simply wish to grow as a person, therapy provides a path forward. Here, you can begin to understand and shift deep-seated patterns that might be holding you back. Our work together will sometimes be challenging as we explore what keeps you stuck, but it can also bring real relief and change.

If you're curious about my approach, my background, or whether therapy might be right for you, please explore the website or contact me via veenstherapeutic@gmail.com for a complimentary initial phone consultation.

What Brings People to Therapy

Life's Big Questions

Many people come to therapy when they're facing fundamental questions about connection, choice, loss, or meaning. Perhaps relationships feel difficult, you're facing a major transition, or life has started to feel empty or overwhelming. Therapy offers space to explore these challenges and develop greater understanding of yourself and what truly matters to you.

Often, we discover that our history directly affects our present relationships and sense of wellbeing. A significant part of our work involves making sense of this past so we can move forward more freely, and weather times of change.

Emotional Difficulties

A path forward

We can work together to address the root causes of anxiety disorders, depression, burn-our, complex trauma or personality disorders. I recognize the important role that life circumstances play in how we feel, particularly for those who have experienced migration, questions around identity and masculinity, LGBTQIA+ specific challenges, or carry concerns about climate and the future.
